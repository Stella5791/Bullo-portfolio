Communicating Interculturally

Misunderstandings Across Cultures and Machines

Misunderstandings are structural. They arise from culture, pragmatics, and technology. Treat them as signals that guide design and practice.

TLDR

In healthcare, misunderstandings affect diagnosis and trust. In AI and localisation they flatten nuance and distort tone. In intercultural exchange they reveal that meaning is negotiated. The goal is not to remove misunderstanding but to respond to it with better design and practice.

Where misunderstandings occur

Intercultural

Different norms for directness and politeness lead to pragmatic mismatch.

Healthcare

Intensity weakens in translation. Patients risk being under treated.

AI and MT

Training on dominant norms flattens sociopragmatic cues and tone.

Business and L10n

Copy travels without its cultural logic and fails in local markets.

Key phenomena

Pragmatic mismatch

Intent and interpretation do not align across cultures.

Semantic attenuation

Meaning loses intensity in translation. Me duele becomes it hurts.

Dataset bias

Dominant varieties crowd out dialects and hybrid styles.

Opaque translation

Outputs feel fluent but hide social meaning and stance.

Consequences

1
Clinical risk
Underestimation of symptoms and delayed care.
2
Loss of trust
Patients and users feel unheard or misread.
3
Brand failure
Copy misses tone and harms local reception.
4
Design drift
Systems optimise for fluency rather than meaning.

What we should do

Healthcare

Train for pragmatic force. Build tools that flag intensity mismatch. Treat patient metaphors as clinical signals.

AI and translation

Broaden data. Add pragmatic evaluation. Test in situated use. Read misunderstandings as redesign prompts.

Business and L10n

Localise strategy not only words. Co create with local speakers. Validate tone, formality, and indirectness.

Takeaway. Misunderstandings are signals. They show where meaning shifts and where culture demands attention.
