Pragmatic Localisation Checklist

By Dr. Stella Bullo

This checklist grew out of my years working as an academic linguist analysing how people build meaning across cultures, and how meaning sometimes slips.

In conversation analysis and intercultural pragmatics, I often saw how misunderstandings are not random errors but structural phenomena, born from mismatched expectations about tone, directness, or politeness. When I moved into AI and localisation work, I recognised the same patterns reappearing inside machine-translated text, UX copy, and healthcare content.

Where a Spanish patient said “me duele” and an English system reduced it to “it hurts”, I recognised semantic attenuation, the same phenomenon I once studied in multilingual discourse data.

I created this checklist to give localisers a tool to spot where pragmatic meaning has been lost, and to restore it before it reaches the user.

Pragmatic Context Review

Closing reflection

Using this checklist helps ensure that localised content carries not only accurate words but also the cultural logic and pragmatic force of the original. It embodies my approach to localisation as a practice of listening across cultures, not just translating between languages.
