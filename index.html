Story

From academic linguistics to applied language technology.

I trained as a linguist in the UK, completing a PhD with a focus on evaluation, metaphor, and how people express experience in context. My research explored how pain, emotion, and stance appear in language — from interviews and forums to clinical encounters.

Over time, my work moved from analysing texts to asking how these insights could be used in tools: how a taxonomy of metaphors might support communication in clinics, how annotation guidelines could improve data quality, and how we might design systems that “explain” their own logic.

Today, I work at the intersection of linguistics, annotation, and NLP. I build frameworks that respect the nuance of language while making it possible to connect human narratives with structured, interpretable data.
