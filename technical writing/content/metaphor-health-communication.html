Explaining Pain with Metaphor: A Practical Guide for Clinicians and Builders

By Stella Bullo · 2025-08-29

Overview

TLDR

  • Patients use metaphors to make pain legible.
  • We map those metaphors to clinical language.
  • We keep patients’ words while adding clarity.
  • This improves empathy and triage.

I study how people talk about pain. I treat metaphors as data. I group expressions into types. I align them with clinical notes, without erasing the original voice.

Who this is for

Clinicians, product teams in digital health, and researchers who need structured language for assessment.

What you will learn

  • Why metaphors matter in consultations
  • Typical patterns in pelvic pain
  • How to translate safely into notes

Step by step

  1. Collect authentic phrases.
  2. Group by type and trigger.
  3. Write a neutral rephrasing that keeps meaning.
  4. Add guidance flags for red routes.

Examples

It feels like glass becomes a sharp, localized pain description, with guidance to check for cyclical patterns and associated symptoms.

Pitfalls

  • Over-interpreting a single phrase
  • Flattening the narrative
  • Ignoring life impact clues

Conclusion

The language is the signal. When we respect it, care becomes faster and kinder.
