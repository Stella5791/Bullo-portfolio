Like a Fire in My Womb

What pain metaphors can reveal about invisible illnesses like endometriosis

“It’s like something is stabbing me from the inside.”
“It feels like an animal is clawing at my belly from within.”
“It’s fire. It’s pressure. It’s something that won’t let me live.”

These are just some of the phrases I’ve heard over the years from other women living with endometriosis. And in my case, the one I repeated most often was: “It feels like someone is strangling my uterus.”
